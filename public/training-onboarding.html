The Premise

Most industries are transformed by technology.
HOA management hasn't been.

Yet.

My Path
CPA→ Audit Partner→ Hedge Fund Ops→ Bedrock→ trustEd

I spent fifteen years chasing the wrong scoreboard. Audit partner. Hedge fund. Six-figure deals.

I eventually realized I wanted to build something that mattered — not just to me, but to the people on the other end of every email, every assessment, every violation letter.

HOA management looked broken from a distance. Up close, it was worse. Good people trapped in bad systems. Homeowners treated like account numbers. Boards drowning in PDFs. Staff burning out because every problem had to be solved twice — once by someone who'd just left.

So I bought Bedrock and started rebuilding the whole thing.

The Opening

Amazon didn't beat Sears
because they had more money.

They beat them because every dollar Sears invested in e-commerce was cannibalizing a store, a vendor relationship, a sales rep's commission. Legacy revenue kept them honest about what they could disrupt.

We have no installed base of operators to protect from automation. No legacy software stack to defend. No consulting revenue that AI would gut. No print-mail processors on retainer.

We can build the right answer instead of the polite answer.

The HOA industry is forty years into running the Sears playbook. We are the company built after the web came out.

The Bedrock translation: every decision starts with the homeowner, the board, or you. Not the contract. Not the policy. Not what's easiest internally. The customer-experience question comes first, every time.

Homeowners don't care about AI. They care that the answer they need shows up in their inbox within an hour and reads like a person wrote it.

Your Role

You are not doing clerical work.

You are supervising a system that's doing the clerical work. Your job is the judgment layer — the moment of decision, the human read on a situation, the choice to override or approve.

Every email you correct, every letter you approve, every call you take — you are training the platform. You're not just doing your job. You're literally building the company.

Why You're Here

We hired you for three things.

Potential
Not a résumé. Not a credential. The capacity to grow into a role that doesn't fully exist yet.
Character
How you'll treat a homeowner having the worst week of their year. We can train the rest.
Curiosity
The question "why does it work this way?" — asked often, with intent, by people who are willing to find out.
Our Standard

Every homeowner is having a moment.

Every call. Every email. Every letter. Somebody on the other end is dealing with something — a fence, a fee, a fine, a family situation that bled into the property. They're not a ticket number. They're a neighbor reaching out, often at a hard moment.

Dignity through operational certainty. Nothing falls through cracks. Nothing is forgotten. Nothing requires a homeowner to chase us. That's the standard.
