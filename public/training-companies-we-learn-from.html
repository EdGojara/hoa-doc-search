Bedrock Association Management
Lesson 1

Amazon

Customer Obsession.
Being customer-focused allows you to be more pioneering.
What we borrow
  • Start with the homeowner and work backwards
  • Treat every complaint as data, not noise
  • Measure friction relentlessly
  • Eliminate recurring pain points through automation
The Bedrock translation
Every homeowner complaint becomes a system improvement. Every board frustration becomes a trustEd feature.
Lesson 2

Apple

Simplicity is the product.

Apple's genius isn't technology. It's removing complexity so completely that customers forget it exists.

What we borrow
  • Remove steps
  • Simplify language
  • Hide complexity
  • Make interactions feel effortless
The Bedrock goal
"I don't know how Bedrock does it, but everything just works."
Lesson 3

Toyota

Kaizen.

Toyota's philosophy of continuous improvement transformed manufacturing. Their principle: anyone can stop the line if they see a problem.

What we borrow
  • Any employee can identify a broken process
  • Problems are surfaced, not hidden
  • Mistakes become learning opportunities, not embarrassments
The Bedrock translation
You are never just doing your job. You are improving the system.
Lesson 4

Ritz-Carlton

Service Recovery.

Ritz-Carlton is legendary for one counterintuitive truth: a complaint handled exceptionally well creates more loyalty than if nothing went wrong.

What we borrow
  • Every complaint is an opportunity to earn trust
  • Empower people to fix problems immediately
  • Don't defend. Recover.
The Bedrock goal
A board member who experienced a problem but was handled well becomes our strongest advocate.
Lesson 5

Southwest Airlines

Happy employees create happy customers.

Southwest understood something most companies miss: you cannot build a customer-obsessed culture on top of a miserable team.

What we borrow
  • Empower employees to solve problems without excessive bureaucracy
  • Reward initiative, not just compliance
  • Celebrate people who improve processes
The Bedrock translation
You have authority to fix small problems immediately. You don't need to ask permission to do the right thing.
Lesson 6

Disney

How do we make people feel?

Disney doesn't just design parks. They design emotions. They ask one question before every decision: how do we make people feel?

HOA management is emotional. Homeowners are frustrated. Boards are stressed. How a homeowner feels after interacting with us may matter as much as what we actually did.

The Bedrock translation
Boards and homeowners are not interruptions. They are guests of your company.
Lesson 7

HubSpot

Close the loop.

HubSpot built its growth around one discipline: capture feedback. Categorize it. Fix it. Tell the customer you fixed it.

You suggested this. We implemented it.

People love seeing their feedback matter.

The Bedrock translation
When a homeowner or board member surfaces a problem that leads to a system improvement, they should know it.
