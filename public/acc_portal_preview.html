Concerns identified — likely to be denied as currently submitted

We received your fence application. Based on Eaglewood's architectural guidelines, the following concerns were identified that may prevent formal approval.

Reference EAG-ARC-2026-0042

Specific concerns

  • Proposed fence height (8 ft) exceeds community maximum — Eaglewood limits exterior fence height to 6 ft (Rules & Regulations Article V §3). A height variance would require board approval as an exception.
  • Vinyl is not on the approved material list — approved fence materials for Eaglewood are cedar, redwood, composite, and wrought iron (Design Standards §4.2). Vinyl is not currently approved.

Most applications with concerns like these can be approved after adjustment — for example, reducing the height to 6 ft and switching to an approved material would resolve both items. You're welcome to revise and resubmit at no cost.

Your two options

Per Texas Property Code §209.00505, you have the right to submit your application as-is for formal Architectural Control Committee review. The committee will respond within 30 days with a written decision — which, based on the guidelines above, is likely to be a denial. You may also revise and resubmit at no cost.

Note on revising: Revising your application doesn't restart any clock or affect your standing. Most owners in this situation choose to adjust the application rather than wait for a formal denial. We're here to help if you have questions about the guidelines.
