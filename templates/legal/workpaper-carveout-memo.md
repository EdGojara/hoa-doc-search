# Memo: Records handover + workpaper carve-out — proposed standard language for Bedrock management agreements

**To:** [HOA attorney — fill in name]
**From:** Ed Gojara, Bedrock Association Management
**Date:** May 22, 2026
**Re:** Strengthening the records-on-termination clause in Bedrock's standard HOA management agreement to (1) define what gets handed over, (2) explicitly carve out Bedrock's workpaper, and (3) set a defensible delivery standard

---

## 1. Why I'm asking

When Bedrock takes over a community from a prior manager, we routinely receive an incomplete records package — sometimes by omission, sometimes by spite, sometimes because the outgoing manager genuinely doesn't know what counts as the association's property versus their own internal materials. I want Bedrock to be the model on the outbound side, both because it's right and because being the clean-export operator is a competitive differentiator with boards who have lived through a sloppy predecessor.

To do that defensibly I need our management agreement to (a) define exactly what Bedrock returns on termination, (b) carve out workpaper as Bedrock's IP, and (c) give us a defined timeline so we can build the export tooling around it.

## 2. The framework I'd like the agreement to reflect

The CPA / attorney parallel is clean: the client owns the original documents and the deliverables; the professional owns the workpaper. I want the same logic codified for our HOA work.

**Association records — Bedrock returns to the Association on termination:**

- Governing documents (CC&Rs, bylaws, rules & regs, plats, dedicatory instruments)
- Board meeting minutes (regular, special, annual, executive-session summaries) and annual meeting records (notices, sign-in/quorum evidence, proxies, ballots, tally)
- Financial records: general ledger, AR aging, AP, bank statements, reconciliations, audited financials, tax returns, 1099s
- Reserve study report and reserve fund history
- Current member roster (owners, mailing addresses, emails on file)
- Executed contracts to which the Association is a party (vendor, insurance, banking)
- Insurance policies and claims history
- Correspondence sent on behalf of the Association (compliance letters issued, board emails to homeowners)
- ARC file: applications submitted, decisions issued, builder approval letters
- Vendor invoices paid with Association funds
- Litigation files involving the Association
- Any document required by Tex. Prop. Code §209.005 to be available for member inspection

**Bedrock workpaper — Bedrock retains as proprietary, not transferable:**

- Internal Bedrock playbook entries and strategic notes
- AI-generated judgment outputs (multi-persona lens analyses, triangulations, draft recommendations)
- Bedrock's internal training materials (Bedrock University modules)
- Portfolio-wide benchmarks (e.g., comparing landscape pricing across Bedrock communities)
- Internal staff notes (private observations, time tracking)
- Draft letters not sent
- Email-triage AI classifications and internal tagging
- The Bedrock platform itself (the trustEd software, AI prompts, memory layer)
- Bedrock's internal vendor evaluation scoring across the portfolio

**Mixed materials — apply the delivery-line rule:**

When the same source material spawns both a deliverable and internal analysis, the delivered artifact is association property; the underlying analysis is workpaper. Examples:

- The vendor recommendation memo *delivered to the board* → Association record. The supporting AI lens analysis → workpaper.
- The board packet *delivered to the board* → Association record. The portfolio-wide data Bedrock cross-referenced → workpaper.
- The compliance letter *sent to a homeowner* → Association record. The drafting history → workpaper.
- The vendor contract PDF (source document) → Association record. Bedrock's structured AI extraction of it → workpaper.

## 3. The specific clause I'd like you to redline

The current standard records-on-termination clause uses the phrase "all records relating to the Association," which I think is dangerously broad. I'd like a redlined version that:

1. **Defines "Association Records"** as a closed list along the lines of Section 2 above (with the §209.005 language tracking the statute exactly).
2. **Defines "Manager Workpaper"** as Bedrock's analyses, internal notes, proprietary software, AI-generated outputs, training materials, portfolio benchmarks, and any other materials developed by Bedrock for its own management processes — explicitly NOT included in the records transfer.
3. **Applies the delivery-line rule** for mixed materials.
4. **Sets a delivery standard**: Bedrock will deliver the Association Records to the Association or its successor manager within 30 days of contract termination, organized by category, with an index, in PDF or native format as appropriate. Bedrock may charge reasonable cost for media and shipping but no fee for the records themselves.
5. **Preserves Bedrock's right to retain copies** of Association Records for the period required by professional standards or applicable law (e.g., financial records retained for 7 years for audit support), subject to ordinary confidentiality.
6. **Survives termination** (the records clause is a surviving obligation, not extinguished by termination for any reason).

## 4. A few specific questions

- Does Tex. Prop. Code §209.005 (member inspection rights) impose any obligations on Bedrock as records *custodian* that I should be aware of, separate from the management agreement?
- Is there a statute or case in Texas that pre-empts the workpaper carve-out (i.e., that says everything in a manager's possession is association property regardless of contract language)? My research says no, but you'll know better.
- For mixed-status materials, is the "delivered = theirs, internal = ours" rule defensible in front of a Texas judge? Or do you recommend a different line?
- Are there records I've listed as workpaper that you'd push back on (e.g., should portfolio-wide vendor pricing benchmarks be considered association property if the Association paid the vendor)?

## 5. What I need from you

A redlined version of the records-and-termination clause in the standard Bedrock management agreement reflecting the above, plus your answers to Section 4. I'd like to roll this out to all communities at the next contract anniversary.
